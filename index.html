Debt Lawsuit Answer Preparation

The Situation

A lawsuit has been filed against you by a debt buyer. Under Texas law, failing to file a written response by the deadline typically results in a default judgment. A default judgment often enables creditors to pursue bank garnishment or receivership.

The Solution

We prepare an Original Answer based on the information you provide, formatted for Texas courts. This document is commonly filed to deny the allegations and request that the plaintiff provide evidence in court. Once we prepare it, you file it with the court clerk.

Bank Garnishment Documents

The Situation

A court order has frozen funds in your bank account. Texas law provides specific exemptions for certain types of income (such as Social Security or child support), but these exemptions must be actively claimed by filing the correct paperwork with the court.

The Solution

We prepare a Claim of Exemption document using your details. This form is used to notify the court and the bank of any protected funds in your account. You obtain the document from us, sign it, and file it to request a hearing.

Eviction Appeal

The Situation

A judgment for possession has been issued in Justice Court. In Texas, tenants generally have 5 days to file an appeal. If no appeal is filed within this strict window, the landlord may request a Writ of Possession to reclaim the property.

The Solution

We prepare a Statement of Inability to Afford Payment of Court Costs (Pauper's Affidavit) for your appeal. This document is commonly used to transfer the case to County Court without immediate payment of the appeal bond. We ensure the document is complete and ready for your submission.
